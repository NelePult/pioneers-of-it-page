Alan Turing

1912–1954

Alan Turing is considered one of the fathers of modern computer science.

With the Turing machine, he laid the theoretical foundations for computing and played a crucial role in breaking the Enigma codes during World War II.

Grace Hopper

1906–1992

Grace Hopper was a programming pioneer who significantly contributed to the development of the first compiler, making programming languages understandable to computers.

She was instrumental in the creation of COBOL, one of the first high-level languages, and popularized the term “bug” for programming errors.

John von Neumann

1903–1957

Mathematician John von Neumann developed the von Neumann architecture, which remains the foundation for most modern computers.

His ideas on memory organization and binary-coded instructions are central concepts in today’s computing systems.

Tim Berners-Lee

born 1955

Tim Berners-Lee is the inventor of the World Wide Web.

In the 1980s he developed the first concepts for hypertext and the HTTP protocol.

Linus Torvalds

born 1969

Linus Torvalds is the creator of the Linux kernel, which serves as the foundation for numerous operating systems.

His open-source philosophy has profoundly shaped software development, enabling global collaboration among developers. Today, Linux powers servers, smartphones, and countless other technological devices.
